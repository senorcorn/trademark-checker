FrameCam Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 15, 2026

FrameCam (“the app”, “we”, “us”) is a camera app that helps you compose photos using on-screen framing guides. This policy explains what data is involved when you use FrameCam and how it is handled.

The short version

  • FrameCam has no accounts and no servers of our own.
  • Your photos and templates stay on your device — we never upload them or see them.
  • The only data leaving your device is what Google AdMob collects to show ads.

Photos and content

  • Photos you capture or import, and the outline images FrameCam generates from them, are stored locally on your device and in your photo library (with your permission). They are not uploaded to us or any third party.
  • Frames and templates you save are stored locally on your device only.
  • We do not have access to, collect, or transmit your photos.

Permissions we request

  • Camera — to show the live preview and take photos.
  • Photo Library — to save your photos and to let you import an existing photo as a framing template.

Advertising

FrameCam shows ads using Google AdMob. To serve and measure ads, Google may collect and process certain data, which can include:

  • Device identifiers (such as the advertising identifier / IDFA),
  • IP address and general (coarse) location derived from it,
  • App usage and diagnostic information.

This data is collected and used by Google in accordance with its own policies: Google Privacy Policy and How Google uses information from apps that use its services.

We request non-personalized ads where possible. On iOS, the app asks for your permission before any tracking (App Tracking Transparency); you can decline, and in the European Economic Area we present a consent form (Google UMP) as required.

Children

FrameCam is not directed to children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your region), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Our AdMob integration is configured as not child-directed.

Data sharing and sale

We do not sell your personal information. The only third party that receives data is Google, as described above.

Your choices

  • iOS: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking to allow or deny tracking; reset your advertising identifier under Apple Advertising / Tracking.
  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads to reset or opt out of ad personalization.
  • You can revoke Camera or Photos access anytime in device settings (some features will stop working).

Your rights (GDPR / CCPA)

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, or delete personal data, or opt out of certain processing. Because FrameCam does not collect or store personal data on a server, requests relating to ad data should be directed to Google. You may also contact us below.

Data security

Your photos and settings remain on your device under the protection of your device’s operating system. We do not operate servers that store your content.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the “Effective date” above and posting the new version at this URL.
